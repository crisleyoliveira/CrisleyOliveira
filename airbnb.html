Impact of Airbnb and Real Estate Speculation

Relationship between Airbnb and real estate speculation in the cities of Lisbon and Rio de Janeiro

Contextualization

The investigation revolves around the evolving real estate dynamics in Lisbon and Rio de Janeiro, with a specific emphasis on the influence of Airbnb on rental markets.

The primary hypothesis guiding this research is that the proliferation of Airbnb licenses may be correlated with alterations in long-term rental prices. The problem at hand centers on understanding the intricate relationship between the surge in short-term accommodation offerings and its potential impact on the availability and pricing of long-term rentals.

This study employs spatial correlation analyses, exploring key features such as property size, number of rooms, and average prices, to unravel the complex interplay between Airbnb activity and traditional rental markets in these two vibrant cities.

Findings and Hypothesis Confirmation

The analyses reveal a noteworthy correlation between the number of licensed Airbnb properties and long-term rental prices.

The spatial correlation analyses demonstrate distinct patterns, with strong positive correlations in certain regions, potentially indicating a relationship between Airbnb activity and rising rental costs.

The investigation also uncovers positive spatial autocorrelation between average long and short-term rental values, further supporting the hypothesis that Airbnb presence might impact traditional rental markets.

These findings corroborate with similar studies conducted in other global cities, highlighting the relevance of the issue on a broader scale.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the study underscores the intricate relationship between Airbnb operations and traditional rental markets in Lisbon and Rio de Janeiro. The rise in Airbnb licenses is found to be potentially linked to diminished availability of permanent housing, contributing to an upsurge in rental prices.

The appreciation of certain areas due to high demand for short-term accommodations further exacerbates the overall increase in rental costs. However, it is essential to acknowledge the complexity of the phenomenon, considering the nuanced spatial patterns and the influence of various factors on the observed correlations.

Recommendations and Solutions

In light of the findings, it is recommended that both cities consider implementing regulatory measures to manage the impact of Airbnb on their housing markets.

Regulation can help strike a balance between the economic benefits of short-term rentals and the potential negative consequences on long-term housing availability and affordability.

Moreover, public education initiatives are crucial to inform the community about the implications of Airbnb on real estate markets, fostering a better understanding of the dynamics involved.

These recommendations emphasize the need for proactive measures to ensure sustainable urban development and to protect the well-being of local communities amidst the evolving landscape of the sharing economy.
