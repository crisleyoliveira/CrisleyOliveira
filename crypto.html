Contextulization

Bitcoin was the first decentralized blockchain-based cryptocurrency, heralding the emergence of cryptocurrencies as a digital medium of exchange in 2009. However, even after 13 years on the market and significant growth in its user base, cryptocurrencies are still far from achieving unanimous acceptance or becoming a popular option.

In the United States, Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies began to gain prominence and adoption in the 2010s. However, government regulation and oversight by agencies such as the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) demonstrate that cryptocurrencies are still in the process of being integrated into the traditional financial system and face considerable regulatory challenges.

Likewise, in Brazil, the cryptocurrency scene started to develop more extensively in the mid-2010s. Although Bitcoin has gained popularity among Brazilian investors, there is still a long way to go for cryptocurrencies to be widely adopted. In December 2022, Brazil approved a legislation, which comes into force in July 2023, providing a safer environment for investors.

Insights

According to the recently published 2023 Germini report, Brazil has emerged as a prominent leader in cryptocurrency adoption in Latin America. The study not only highlights the current situation in Brazil, but also indicates a promising future for cryptocurrency adoption in the country, with at least 33% of respondents intending to acquire cryptocurrencies in the next 12 months.

In 2021, Brazil recorded a cryptocurrency adoption rate of approximately 4.9%, with only 8% of holders identifying as female. However, significant progress has been made in 2022, witnessing nearly 3% growth with 7.8% cryptocurrency holders in just 12 months. Notably, almost half of these holders are women, representing approximately 45% of the total. This encouraging trend underscores the growing participation of women in the Brazilian cryptocurrency scene.

Furthermore, the report highlights that Latin America as a whole currently leads globally in terms of female cryptocurrency ownership, with around 43% of all cryptocurrency owners in the region being women, surpassing the percentages seen in other parts of the world. This finding may demonstrate a more inclusive nature of the cryptocurrency market in Latin America.

Gender is a significant factor that stands out in these reports, pointing to women in both countries as the most significant number of crypto-curious individuals. Both in Brazil and in the United States, women have shown more curiosity and openness to acquiring cryptocurrencies in the next 12 months.

Despite progress and growing interest, it is important to recognize that cryptocurrencies still face challenges such as price volatility, security concerns, and complex regulatory issues in both countries. Therefore, although they have gained ground and generated interest over the years, there is still a long way to go for cryptocurrencies to become widely accepted investment options and means of exchange in society as a whole.
