Top 100 videogames of all time

In this project, we will analyze the top 100 video games of all time based on their rating, popularity, and release year. The data set used for this project will be obtained from a reputable online video game database. The aim of this project is to identify patterns and trends among the top-rated video games and to uncover insights that can help inform game development and marketing decisions.

Normalization is used to standardize data within a specific range, making it easier to compare variables with different scales. In this visualization, the Metascore and User Score columns were normalized using the min-max technique, scaling values between 0 and 1. Metascore has a smaller original scale (94 to 99) compared to User Score (7.5 to 9.3), so its normalized values will be smaller. While Metascore has higher averages, the normalized averages appear lower. However, the normalization process doesn't change the relative relationships between the data, just places them on a common scale for easier comparison.

âœ… 80% of the top 50 bestselling games are not in the top 100 by critics

There's a big difference between popular games in terms of sales and how critics perceive them. Games like Minecraft, PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds and Tetris have sold millions of copies but aren't highly rated by critics. This shows that the general public and critics have different standards when evaluating games. Best-selling games focus on broad appeal and factors like accessibility and social aspects, while critical acclaim is influenced by more subjective criteria. It's important to remember that commercial success doesn't always equal critical recognition in the gaming industry.
