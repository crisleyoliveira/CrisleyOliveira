And do different gaming brands and platforms have a significant impact on these dynamics? Prepare to have your preconceptions challenged and your curiosity satiated as we unravel the mysteries that lie beneath the surface of the gaming industry. The journey awaits, and the answers lie within the numbers.

In this visualization, the normalized mean Metascore and User Score for different brands are compared. The brands included in the visualization are Microsoft, Nintendo, PC, SEGA, and Sony.

The Metascore is a score assigned to a video game based on reviews from professional critics, while the User Score represents the average rating given by users or players. These scores provide an indication of the overall reception and quality of the games associated with each brand.

By normalizing the scores using the min-max technique, all the values are scaled between 0 and 1, making it easier to compare them on a common scale. Normalization doesn't change the relative relationships between the data points, but rather places them within a standardized range.

From the normalized scores, we can observe the relative performance of each brand based on the average scores. SEGA has the highest normalized Metascore (0.9733), indicating that its games, on average, received higher ratings from critics compared to the other brands. Nintendo has the highest normalized User Score (0.881), suggesting that users or players rated their games more positively on average.

Overall, this visualization provides insights into the comparative performance of different gaming brands based on their average scores, both from critics and users.
